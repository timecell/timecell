# Bitcoin Investing Framework — v2

**Purpose:** The complete decision framework for someone who owns (or is considering owning) Bitcoin as an investor, not a speculator. Extracted from lived experience managing a concentrated Bitcoin position across multiple cycles.

**Audience:** Anyone from "I'm curious about Bitcoin" to "I have significant Bitcoin and need systems around it."

**Related articles:** Each section links to published articles at masterclassbitcoin.com for deeper reading.

## Part 1: Why Bitcoin

*Before you size a position, you need to understand what you're buying.*

### 1.1 What Problem Does Bitcoin Solve?

Every other store-of-value asset has a fatal flaw:
- **Cash/USD:** Unlimited supply. Your government decides how much your money is worth.
- **Gold:** Limited supply, but hard to verify, expensive to move, impossible to divide easily.
- **Bonds:** Government IOUs denominated in the currency they can print.
- **Real estate:** Illiquid, high carry cost (tax, maintenance), tied to local politics.
- **Stocks:** Elastic supply (issuance, buybacks), entirely dependent on management and regulation.

Bitcoin fixes: mathematically fixed supply (21M), perfectly verifiable, instantly portable, near-zero carry cost, completely independent of any government or company.

→ *Read more: [Why Bitcoin, Why Now?](https://www.masterclassbitcoin.com/why-bitcoin-why-now/) · [The Three Eras of Money](https://www.masterclassbitcoin.com/the-three-eras-of-money/)*

### 1.2 The Store-of-Value Scorecard

Score any asset on 8 properties:

| Property | What It Means | Bitcoin |
|----------|--------------|---------|
| **Scarcity** | Can more be created? | Fixed 21M — best possible score |
| **Policy Independence** | Can a government change the rules? | Total — no one controls it |
| **Carry Cost** | What does it cost to hold? | Near-zero (a USB drive or a seed phrase) |
| **Portability** | Can you move it across borders? | Perfect — $1B moves in 10 minutes for $2 |
| **Verifiability** | Can you prove it's real? | Perfect — every transaction is mathematically verified |
| **Yield** | Does it produce income? | None needed — it IS money, not a productive asset |
| **Speculative Layer** | Is price driven by speculation? | High but declining as institutional adoption grows |
| **Monetary Integrity** | Can the rules themselves be changed? | Mathematical — no human can alter the protocol |

**Note on Scarcity vs Monetary Integrity:** Scarcity answers "can more be created?" (the number). Monetary Integrity answers "can the rules that protect that number be changed?" (the governance). One is the promise, the other is what enforces the promise.

Bitcoin isn't competing with Apple stock or Mumbai real estate. It's competing with gold, government bonds, and cash as a store of value. That's a $900 trillion market. Bitcoin is currently about 1% of it.

→ *Read more: [Bitcoin's Real Value: Store of Value, Not Cost Reduction](https://www.masterclassbitcoin.com/bitcoins-real-value-store-of-value-not-cost-reduction/) · [Fixed Supply Creates Volatility. Flexible Supply Hides Theft.](https://www.masterclassbitcoin.com/fixed-supply-creates-volatility-flexible-supply-hides-theft/)*

### 1.3 Thesis Health Check

The scorecard is a snapshot. The real world sends signals over time that either reinforce or weaken each property. Periodically ask yourself: is each property getting stronger or weaker?

**Strengthening signals (examples):**
- Nation-state adoption (El Salvador, US Strategic Reserve)
- ETF AUM growth (IBIT became a top 10 BlackRock ETF within 12 months)
- Hash rate at all-time highs (network security increasing)
- No fork has ever successfully changed Bitcoin's 21M supply — every attempt has failed

**What would weaken the thesis:**
- A fundamental break in the protocol (supply change, cryptographic failure)
- Coordinated global ban that actually works (not just talk)
- Sustained network centralization (mining pools >51%)
- Quantum computing breakthrough (currently 100 qubits vs 1.9 billion needed)

**Rule of thumb:** If you can't articulate whether the thesis is getting stronger or weaker, you're not paying attention. If the answer is "weaker" on 2+ properties, revisit your conviction rung (see Part 2).

## Part 2: The Conviction Ladder

Understanding deepens in stages. You can't skip rungs — each one unlocks the next.

| Rung | Allocation | Label | What You Believe | What Moves You Up |
|------|-----------|-------|-----------------|-------------------|
| 1 | 0% | **Observer** | "It might be interesting" | Reading, conversation, studying the scorecard |
| 2 | 1-3% | **Experimenter** | "I'll put a little in to learn" | Buying your first Bitcoin, surviving your first -30% drop |
| 3 | 5-10% | **Diversifier** | "I understand why this exists" | Studying the scorecard deeply, understanding fixed supply vs fiat printing |
| 4 | 10-25% | **High Conviction** | "This is a core portfolio position" | Living through a full cycle (crash + recovery), seeing the 4-year pattern |
| 5 | 25-50% | **Owner-Class** | "Bitcoin anchors my portfolio" | All gates below must pass |
| 6 | 50%+ | **Single-Asset Core** | "This is my monetary foundation" | All gates below + downside insurance in place |

**Critical rule:** Your allocation should match your conviction, not your greed. If you can't explain WHY you hold Bitcoin in 2 sentences, your position is too large.

### What Each Rung Looks Like

**Rung 2 — Experimenter:** A business owner who heard about Bitcoin from a friend. Puts $5K in to "see what happens." Checks the price daily. Doesn't yet understand why fixed supply matters. When it drops 25%, they feel nervous but hold because the amount is small enough not to matter.

**Rung 3 — Diversifier:** A professional with a diversified portfolio who's read enough to understand the store-of-value thesis. Allocates 7% of their investment portfolio — enough to matter if Bitcoin succeeds, small enough to absorb if it fails. Has other investments they're comfortable with.

**Rung 4 — High Conviction:** Someone who's held through a full crash-and-recovery cycle. They've watched their Bitcoin drop 60% and come back to new highs. They now understand viscerally that volatility is the admission fee. Bitcoin is a core position alongside their other assets. They've started thinking about custody.

**Rung 5 — Owner-Class:** A concentrated holder who has built infrastructure around the position — liquidity reserve covering 2+ years of expenses, systematic selling rules, proper custody. They think in terms of portfolio structure, not just allocation percentage. Their non-Bitcoin assets are deliberately uncorrelated.

**Rung 6 — Single-Asset Core:** Someone whose wealth is primarily in Bitcoin by conviction, not by accident. They have downside insurance in place, a selling ladder tied to market conditions, estate planning that accounts for the position, and a team (accountant, legal) that understands their structure. Their life is designed around holding through any cycle.

### Gates for Rungs 5-6 (25%+)

These are non-negotiable prerequisites, not suggestions:

1. **Multi-cycle experience.** You've survived at least one 70%+ drawdown without selling. Conviction earned through volatility, not borrowed from a podcast.
2. **Liquidity backstop.** 2+ years of living expenses held OUTSIDE Bitcoin. This money is untouchable.
3. **No forced-sale liabilities.** No margin, no debt covenants, no looming tax bills that force liquidation at the wrong time.
4. **Sleep test at -80%.** Mark your portfolio to market at -80%. Does your life actually change? If yes, you're too concentrated.
5. **Written de-risk triggers.** Pre-committed rules for when and how you'll reduce — not "I'll figure it out when it happens."

**Additional gate for Rung 6 (50%+):**

6. **Downside insurance in place.** Budget 1-3% annually for protective put options (see Part 6). At this concentration, you're not just an investor — you're a portfolio manager.

### Re-entry Protocol

If you previously held Bitcoin and sold — whether at a loss or prematurely — you're not starting at Observer. You already have conviction. Your problem is timing shame.

- Start at Experimenter (Rung 2) regardless of past experience
- Use DCA to re-enter — do NOT try to "time it better this time"
- Do NOT anchor to your exit price. The market doesn't know or care what you sold at.
- Temperature tells you where the market is NOW, not where it was when you left

## Part 3: Position Sizing Process

Position sizing is not a single number — it's a 6-step process.

### Step 1: Identify Your Conviction Rung

Where are you on the Conviction Ladder (Part 2)? This sets your nominal allocation range.

### Step 2: Apply the Capacity Gate

Your conviction says what you'd LIKE to allocate. Your capacity says what you CAN allocate.

**Effective Allocation = min(Conviction Rung, Risk Capacity Ceiling)**

Capacity depends on three things:

- **Human capital remaining.** A 30-year-old with $35K and a $150K salary has millions in future earnings. BTC at 50% of $35K is tuition money — their real capacity is high. A 60-year-old retiree with $3.5M has far less capacity even though the dollar amount is larger.
- **Withdrawal horizon.** Need the money within 5 years? Your capacity drops regardless of conviction. Bitcoin's worst 5-year stretches included 70%+ drawdowns.
- **Liability schedule.** Mortgage payments, school fees, tax obligations that cannot be deferred. These create forced-sale risk. Every dollar committed to a near-term liability cannot be in Bitcoin.

**Retirement constraint:** 10+ years to withdrawal = framework applies normally. 5-10 years = halve your capacity ceiling. Under 5 years = Bitcoin should only come from money genuinely outside retirement math.

### Step 3: Stress Test (The Ruin Test)

Ignore correlation math. One question:

> "If Bitcoin drops 80% AND your other assets drop 40% at the same time — as happened in 2022 — do you survive? Can you meet all obligations?"

If yes: your current rung is fine.
If no: drop one rung until the answer is yes.

**Simple heuristic:** If your non-Bitcoin assets are mostly tech stocks, growth equities, or other crypto — they'll drop WITH Bitcoin during crashes. The diversification you think you have doesn't exist when you need it most. Drop one rung.

### Step 4: Decide on Downside Insurance

- Required for Rung 6 (50%+)
- Recommended for Rung 5 (25-50%)
- Optional for Rungs 3-4
- See Part 6 for details

### Step 5: Write Your Rules

Before entering a position, write down:
- At what Temperature will you start selling? (Not "when I feel like it")
- At what drawdown will you re-evaluate?
- What would make you exit entirely? (Kill criteria — see Part 1.3)
- What will you do with proceeds? (Reinvest, safety net, spend?)

Pre-committed rules are commitment devices against your future panicking self.

### Step 6: Sleep Test

Final check. Take your planned allocation. Imagine an 80% drawdown tomorrow. Calculate the dollar loss. Say it out loud.

If you flinch: reduce until you don't.

## Part 4: Entry and Accumulation

### 4.1 The Temperature Framework

Bitcoin moves in cycles. Rather than trying to predict the future, measure where you are in the current cycle.

**Bitcoin Temperature (0-100):** A composite of two on-chain metrics (MVRV + RHODL) that measures whether Bitcoin is cheap, fair, or expensive relative to its own history.

| Temperature | Zone | What It Means | What To Do |
|-------------|------|--------------|------------|
| 0-20 | Extreme Fear | Historically near cycle bottoms | Maximum conviction buying. This is where generational wealth is built. |
| 20-40 | Fear | Market is scared, below fair value | Accumulate steadily. DCA aggressively. |
| 40-60 | Neutral | Fair value range | Continue DCA at normal pace. Don't try to be clever. |
| 60-75 | Greed | Market is getting heated | Slow down buying. Start thinking about risk management. |
| 75-100 | Extreme Greed | Historically near cycle tops | Stop buying. Activate selling rules. Protect gains. |

Temperature doesn't predict the future. It tells you whether the market is acting scared or greedy right now. When others are scared, you accumulate. When others are greedy, you protect.

→ *Read more: [What Is Bitcoin Temperature?](https://www.masterclassbitcoin.com/what-is-bitcoin-temperature/)*

### 4.2 DCA vs Lump Sum

The research shows lump sum outperforms DCA roughly two-thirds of the time. But the one-third where it doesn't — when you buy near a local top and watch a 40-60% crash — is exactly when most people capitulate and sell at a loss.

**DCA's real advantage isn't mathematical. It's behavioral:** it keeps you in the game.

- **Default:** DCA. Buy a fixed amount on a fixed schedule regardless of price.
- **Temperature as tiebreaker:** Below 30 (extreme fear), the data and the emotions both favor lump sum. Above 50, DCA protects you from yourself.
- **When to pause DCA:** Temperature above 75. Don't buy into euphoria.

→ *Read more: [Dollar Cost Averaging for Bitcoin Accumulation](https://www.masterclassbitcoin.com/dollar-cost-averaging-for-bitcoin-accumulation/)*

### 4.3 The 4-Year Moving Average

Bitcoin's price has never stayed below its 4-year moving average for long. When it does, that's the strongest buy signal that exists. The floor keeps rising — from $19K (2021) to $57K (2026).

When price is near or below the 4-year MA AND Temperature is in Fear territory — that's the highest-conviction entry point the framework offers.

## Part 5: Holding and Selling

### 5.1 Understanding Beats Prediction

Nobody can predict Bitcoin's price. Not you, not analysts, not AI models.

What you CAN do:
- **Understand** where you are in the cycle (Temperature)
- **Understand** your own risk tolerance (Sleep Test)
- **Understand** your liquidity needs (when will you need to sell?)
- **Have a system** that removes emotion from decisions

### 5.2 Volatility Is the Admission Fee

Bitcoin's volatility (50-80% annual) is not a bug. It is the mechanism that produces the returns.

- If Bitcoin were stable, it would be a finished product with no upside
- Every 50% crash in Bitcoin's history has been followed by new all-time highs
- The people who made the most from Bitcoin are the ones who did nothing during crashes

→ *Read more: [Volatility Is the Admission Fee, Not a Punishment](https://www.masterclassbitcoin.com/volatility-is-the-admission-fee-not-a-punishment/) · [Why Surviving the Crash Matters More Than Predicting It](https://www.masterclassbitcoin.com/why-surviving-the-crash-matters-more-than-predicting-it/)*

### 5.3 When to Sell

**Default rule:** Don't sell because the price dropped. Sell systematically, based on Temperature.

**When selling makes sense:**
- Temperature above 75 (market euphoria) — take profits on a schedule, not on emotion
- You need the money for actual life expenses
- Your concentration has grown beyond your Sleep Test comfort

**What's NOT a reason to sell:**
- "It dropped 30% this week" — this is normal
- "Someone on TV said it's going to zero" — this happens every cycle
- "I saw another crypto that's going up faster" — don't chase

**Tax overlay (critical):** Never execute a Temperature-based sell without checking your tax position first. The difference between short-term and long-term capital gains treatment can be 20-40% of the decision's value. A sell that makes sense pre-tax can be destructive after-tax.

## Part 6: Downside Insurance

*Renamed from "hedging" — this is insurance, not trading.*

### 6.1 What It Is

Downside insurance works like homeowner's insurance. You pay a small annual premium (1-3% of your Bitcoin position). Most years, nothing happens and the premium is your cost. But if Bitcoin crashes 70%+, the insurance pays out — giving you the ability to buy more at low prices instead of being forced to sell.

**The mechanism:** Buy deep out-of-the-money put options — contracts that pay you when Bitcoin falls below a set price. You choose how much protection you want and how much premium you're willing to pay.

### 6.2 Why It Matters

The purpose of downside insurance is NOT to "make money on the hedge." It's to improve your long-term compound return (geometric mean).

The math (Spitznagel/Taleb): A small insurance cost that prevents you from panic-selling during crashes more than pays for itself over a decade. The psychological benefit alone — knowing your downside is bounded — lets you hold a larger position with more confidence.

### 6.3 Who Needs It

| Rung | Insurance Status |
|------|-----------------|
| Rungs 1-3 (0-10%) | Optional — position is small enough that a crash doesn't threaten survival |
| Rung 4 (10-25%) | Consider it — especially if your other assets correlate with Bitcoin |
| Rung 5 (25-50%) | Recommended — your portfolio outcome now depends heavily on Bitcoin |
| Rung 6 (50%+) | Required — this is a non-negotiable gate |

### 6.4 What NOT to Do with Insurance

- Don't try to profit from insurance — it's a cost, not a trade
- Don't use leverage to fund insurance — defeats the purpose
- Don't roll insurance during panic — set it up during calm periods
- Don't confuse stop-losses with insurance — stop-losses sell your Bitcoin; insurance pays you while you keep holding

## Part 7: Custody and Security

### 7.1 The Custody Spectrum

| Method | Security | Convenience | Best For |
|--------|----------|-------------|----------|
| Exchange (Coinbase, Kraken) | Medium — you trust a company | High — easy to buy/sell | Beginners, small amounts, active trading |
| Hardware wallet (Ledger, Trezor) | High — you hold your keys | Medium — requires setup and care | Most holders above $10K |
| Multi-sig / Collaborative custody | Very High — multiple keys required | Lower — more complex | Large positions, institutional |

**Every custody method requires a seed phrase backup.** Write your recovery phrase on paper or stamp it on metal. Never store it digitally. Never share it. If you lose it, your Bitcoin is gone forever. There is no customer support. This is not a separate custody type — it's a non-negotiable companion to every method above.

### 7.2 Core Principles

- **"Not your keys, not your coins."** If your Bitcoin is on an exchange, you're trusting that company. FTX, Mt. Gox, and dozens of other exchanges have proven this trust can be misplaced.
- **Self-custody is a skill, not a feature.** Learn it before you need it. Start with a small amount on a hardware wallet.
- **Security scales with position size.** $1K on an exchange is fine. $100K deserves a hardware wallet. $1M+ deserves multi-sig and a custody plan.

## Part 8: The Journey

### 8.1 Stage 1: Learning

*Some people spend a year here. Others compress it into weeks. The milestone matters, not the calendar.*

- You're reading, watching, trying to understand
- Your biggest risk: buying too much before you understand, or dismissing it because someone you respect called it "rat poison squared"
- **Action:** Small position (1-3%), start DCA, read The Bitcoin Standard
- **Milestone to advance:** Survive your first -30% drop without selling

### 8.2 Stage 2: Tested

- You've lived through at least one -30% to -50% correction
- You either sold in panic (most people) or held (the ones who build wealth)
- If you held, your conviction is now earned, not borrowed
- **Action:** If conviction deepened, increase allocation. If you panicked, reduce to a size where you won't panic next time. Neither response is wrong — it's information about your real risk tolerance.
- **Milestone to advance:** Hold through a full cycle (crash AND recovery). See the pattern with your own money at stake.

### 8.3 Stage 3: Systems

- You understand the cycles. You've seen Bitcoin declared dead and come back stronger.
- Your thinking shifts from "should I own Bitcoin?" to "how do I structure my life around this position?"
- **Action:** Build the safety net (barbell). Consider downside insurance. Create selling rules (Temperature-based, not emotion-based). Think about custody, tax, estate planning, generational transfer.
- **Milestone:** You have written rules and a structure. You don't check the price daily.

### 8.4 The Barbell-to-Buckets Evolution

Most investors start with a barbell: 90% safe (cash, treasuries) + 10% Bitcoin. This is the correct starting structure. It removes prediction from portfolio construction.

As conviction deepens and the position grows, the portfolio evolves into buckets:

| Bucket | Purpose | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| BTC Core | Long-term wealth growth | The Bitcoin you don't sell |
| Safety Net | Living expenses, untouchable | Cash + short-term government bonds |
| Liquidity Reserve | Dry powder for crisis deployments | Treasury ETFs, money market |
| Downside Insurance | Crash protection | Put options (1-3% annual cost) |
| Diversified Assets | Non-Bitcoin investments | Equity index funds, real estate, gold |

The barbell is the entry structure. Buckets are the mature structure. You graduate from one to the other as your understanding and position grow.

### 8.5 Special Situations

**Inherited Bitcoin / Windfall:** If you didn't earn this position through study and surviving volatility, don't rush to sell — but don't assume you should hold either. Your first job is education: study the scorecard, understand the cycles, build YOUR conviction. The biggest risk is acting before you understand — either selling too fast (feels like free money) or holding passively on someone else's conviction. Once you've done the work and placed yourself on the Conviction Ladder honestly, then size accordingly. The Bitcoin itself doesn't need to move while you learn.

**Leverage survivors:** If you were burned by leverage or an exchange collapse (FTX, Luna), your problem was the instrument, not the asset. Bitcoin didn't fail — the intermediary or the strategy did. Start fresh with spot, self-custody, small size. Rebuild trust with the asset by holding through a cycle.

**Jurisdiction matters:** If your Bitcoin holdings cross a meaningful threshold of your net worth, jurisdiction planning deserves dedicated legal advice — before the tax event, not after. A single jurisdiction change can save 20-40% of portfolio value.

### 8.6 This Framework Will Evolve

This framework is calibrated for Bitcoin in its volatile monetization phase — the period where it's transitioning from a speculative asset to a mature store of value. If volatility compresses permanently below 20% (which would mean Bitcoin has largely "won"), the Temperature model, insurance math, and conviction ladder will all need recalibration.

The framework is a living document, not a stone tablet.

## Part 9: Bitcoin vs Other Cryptocurrencies

This framework is about Bitcoin, not "crypto." The distinction matters.

### 9.1 The Price Test

Check the price of any altcoin in Bitcoin (not in dollars). Almost every altcoin follows the same pattern: one initial pump against Bitcoin, followed by a permanent decline. Very few cryptocurrencies have ever made more than one all-time high when priced in BTC.

Why this matters: if an altcoin can't outperform Bitcoin over multiple cycles, you're taking more risk for less return. The dollar price of altcoins can go up during bull markets — but so does Bitcoin, and Bitcoin doesn't go to zero in bear markets.

### 9.2 Why Bitcoin Is Different

- **Network effect:** Bitcoin has the largest, most decentralized, most secure network. This matters for a store of value the same way it matters for a language — the most widely spoken one wins.
- **Lindy effect:** Bitcoin has been running continuously since 2009. Every year it survives makes it more likely to survive the next year. Most altcoins are less than 5 years old.
- **No founder, no foundation, no company.** Bitcoin has no CEO to make bad decisions, no board to change strategy, no foundation to get hacked. It's a protocol, like the internet itself.
- **Different category:** Most altcoins are technology platforms (competing with each other on features). Bitcoin is money (competing with gold, USD, and bonds on monetary properties). Different competition, different analysis.

## Part 10: Common Objections

The same objections have been raised against Bitcoin for 15+ years. Understanding why they're wrong (or right but irrelevant) is part of building conviction.

### "Bitcoin could be hacked"

A 51% attack cannot steal from wallets (requires private keys), cannot create new coins, and can only double-spend recent transactions. After 6 confirmations (about 1 hour), transactions are practically irreversible even with majority hashrate. Estimated cost to attempt: over $5 billion, for a very limited and detectable attack. Bitcoin's security isn't based on trust — it's based on making attacks economically irrational.

### "Quantum computing will break Bitcoin"

Current quantum computers have about 100 physical qubits. Breaking Bitcoin's cryptography (ECDSA signatures) would require approximately 1.9 billion qubits — a gap of 7 orders of magnitude. Bitcoin's hashing algorithm (SHA-256) gets only a quadratic speedup from quantum computing, which is insufficient to break it. NIST finalized post-quantum cryptographic standards in August 2024, and Bitcoin can adopt them through a soft fork if needed — well before quantum computers reach the required scale.

### "Bitcoin uses too much energy"

Bitcoin mining consumes significant energy — by design. That energy expenditure is what makes the network secure. The relevant questions: (a) Is the security worth the cost? For a $1T+ monetary network, the answer is yes. (b) What type of energy? An increasing share comes from stranded, renewable, or otherwise-wasted energy sources, because miners are price-sensitive and seek the cheapest electricity. (c) Compared to what? The traditional banking system, gold mining, and military enforcement of fiat currencies all consume enormous resources — they're just less visible.

### "Governments will ban it"

Some governments have tried (China, India partially). The result: Bitcoin moved to other jurisdictions. You can ban exchanges and on-ramps, making it inconvenient. You cannot ban the protocol itself — it runs on the internet, and transactions can be broadcast from anywhere. The trend is toward regulation and adoption (ETFs, strategic reserves), not bans. Each major government that embraces Bitcoin makes bans by others less effective and less likely.

### "It has no intrinsic value"

Neither does gold. Neither does the US dollar (since 1971). "Intrinsic value" is a concept from commodity analysis that doesn't apply to monetary assets. Money's value comes from collective agreement, network effects, and monetary properties (see the Scorecard in Part 1). By these measures, Bitcoin scores higher than any alternative.

### "It's too volatile to be money"

Volatility is the admission fee for participating in Bitcoin's monetization. As adoption grows and the market matures, volatility will compress — just as it has been compressing cycle over cycle. Gold was volatile too when it was being demonetized from currency to store-of-value. The volatility is the transition, not the destination.

### "It gives no yield"

Correct — and that's by design. Bitcoin is money, not a productive asset. Cash in your safe doesn't pay interest either. Gold doesn't pay dividends. The "yield" on Bitcoin is its long-term appreciation as it captures share of the $900T store-of-value market. If you need income, that comes from other parts of your portfolio (the buckets in Part 8). Bitcoin's job is to be the hardest, most scarce asset you own — not to generate cash flow. Be especially skeptical of anyone offering "yield on your Bitcoin" — that means they're lending it out, which introduces counterparty risk and defeats the purpose of self-custody. Every major Bitcoin yield product (BlockFi, Celsius, Voyager) has failed.

```
    ├── Yes → Stop immediately.
    └── No → Good. Never start.
```
